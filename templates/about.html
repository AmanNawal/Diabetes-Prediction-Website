About Project


Diabetes Predictor is a website that utilizes a combination of HTML, Bootstrap CSS, JavaScript, and Python to predict an individual's risk of developing diabetes. The website's main feature is a predictive algorithm that uses machine learning techniques to analyze a user's data and provide personalized insights into their risk of diabetes. The website's user interface is designed using HTML and Bootstrap CSS, providing a clean and intuitive layout for users to interact with. Users can take a quick and easy risk assessment, which includes questions about their age, family history, and lifestyle habits. The responses are then analyzed by the predictive algorithm, which is implemented in Python.

The algorithm takes into account a wide range of factors, including genetics, physical activity, diet, and other lifestyle choices, to determine the user's risk of developing diabetes. The results are displayed on the website in an easy-to-understand format, along with personalized recommendations for reducing the risk of diabetes.

In addition to the risk assessment feature, the website also provides a wealth of educational resources, including articles, videos, and infographics, to help users understand diabetes and manage their risk. These resources are accessible through a navigation bar at the top of the website, which is implemented using JavaScript.

Overall, Diabetes Predictor is a comprehensive and user-friendly website that utilizes the latest technologies in HTML, Bootstrap CSS, JavaScript, and Python to provide valuable insights and resources for individuals at risk of or living with diabetes.

Acetone

Acetone is a chemical compound that is present in small amounts in the human body. It is produced by the breakdown of fat and can be found in the breath, blood, and urine. When an individual has diabetes, their body is unable to properly use and store glucose, leading to an increase in the breakdown of fat for energy, which results in an increase of acetone in the body. Specifically, in uncontrolled diabetes, the body starts to break down fat for energy instead of glucose, this process is called ketosis. As a result, it produces ketone bodies one of them is Acetone.

Type-1 Diabetes

High levels of acetone in the breath, blood or urine, can be an indication of uncontrolled diabetes, specifically type 1 diabetes, as well as diabetes caused by certain medications or other conditions. Therefore, measuring acetone levels can be used as a diagnostic tool for diabetes.
